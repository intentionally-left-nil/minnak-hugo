The Fairphone is the only smartphone I've owned where the second-year experience is meaningfully better than the first. Not because of software updates, but because the parts I cared about — the battery, the camera — were user-replaceable on a kitchen table.

## What the spec sheet misses

The replaceable battery is the headline feature, but in two years I've only swapped it once. The hidden value is *not* worrying about it. I charge to whatever percentage is convenient, leave it on a charger overnight, abuse it however I want, because the eventual remedy is a 30€ part and a screwdriver, not a new phone.

The camera module upgrade is the more interesting story. Mid-cycle, Fairphone released a new sensor that drops into the existing chassis. Five minutes of disassembly, a firmware sync, and the phone is materially better than the one I bought.

## What it doesn't do

Performance is fine for everyday use and noticeably behind flagships for anything graphically intensive. The cameras are good, not great. The screen is good, not great. None of this matters to me, but it's worth saying.

The sustainability story is the real reason to buy one — and it only works if you actually keep the phone for the full lifecycle. A Fairphone that gets replaced every two years is just an expensive normal phone.
