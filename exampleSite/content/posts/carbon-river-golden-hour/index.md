Shooting the Carbon River section of Mount Rainier National Park at golden hour with a Pentax K1000 loaded with Kodak Portra 400.

{{< figure cover=true >}}

The Carbon River rainforest is the only inland temperate rainforest in the continental United States. It receives 70–80 inches of rain per year, and it shows: moss carpets everything, nurse logs sprout entire ecosystems, the canopy filters light into something almost green.

## The shoot

I arrived at the gate at 5:45 AM, about ninety minutes before official golden hour. The plan was to walk the closed road — washed out years ago, now a gravel trail — far enough to be well inside the forest canopy when the light started.

Portra 400 was the right choice. The film handles mixed light gracefully, the highlights roll off without blowing, and the greens render warm rather than clinical. Shot everything at f/8 to keep the foreground moss sharp.

## What surprised me

The sound. I expected silence but got constant motion — water dripping from every surface, branches creaking, a distant creek. The Carbon River forest is alive in a way that feels more obvious than drier ecosystems.

Also: a Roosevelt elk herd about two miles in. Completely unexpected, entirely worth the early alarm.

## Technical notes

- Camera: Pentax K1000
- Film: Kodak Portra 400 (rated at box speed)
- Lens: 50mm f/1.7 SMC Pentax-M
- Development: Pushed E-6 in C-41 process (cross-process, intentionally)
